## Overview: Project Management Processes at OctoAcme

OctoAcme delivers projects using a lifecycle-driven approach emphasizing customer value, measurable outcomes, and clear accountability. Projects progress through Initiation, Planning, Execution, Release, and Retrospective phases. Initiation captures business need and aligns stakeholders using a one-pager template. Planning breaks work into shippable increments, prioritizes backlogs, defines acceptance criteria, and sets timelines and milestones.

Execution relies on disciplined workflows: work is tracked via GitHub Project boards, employing standard columns (`Backlog`, `Ready`, `In Progress`, `In Review`, `QA`, `Done`) to visualize progress. The team holds daily standups, regular demos, and weekly sync meetings. Pull requests require at least one approval, passing CI, and adherence to coding standards.

Roles are clearly defined: Project Managers coordinate schedules and risk; Product Managers own the backlog and business outcomes; Developers implement features; QA ensures quality; Stakeholders provide feedback and approvals. Risk management and escalation paths are documented, with triage occurring in standups and further escalation as needed. Continuous improvement takes place in structured retrospectives after every milestone, driving actionable improvements and transparency.
